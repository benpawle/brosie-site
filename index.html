Things to think about

FOOD & DRINK


We wanted to fit as much time in for dancing as possible so we will be serving a tasty Greek summer buffet in the late afternoon at the barn. Even though we are eating early, make sure you have a hearty lunch or brunch if you can. We’ll provide wine, beer, and bubbles through the afternoon and there will be a paid bar in the barn for everything else.

Don't forget to add any dietary requirements to your RSVP.

CEILIDH & EVENING GUESTS


The Ceilidh will be kicking off about 8:30pm after the first dance, followed by some party tunes later on. Evening guests please aim to arrive from 8pm onwards. We're looking at putting a bus on at the end of the night, let us know if you need dropped off somewhere.

DRESS CODE


There’s no do's and don'ts but please use this as an excuse to don your party clothes and look fabulous! Most important of all - bring some shoes you can dance in :)

NEXT DAY


We’re continuing the celebrations with a relaxed BBQ on Sunday the 15th July at Rosie’s home. Plenty of tea and cake. Join us from 12pm onwards.

Travel

TRAINS

Carlisle is on the west coast mainline so easy to get to from London Euston and Glasgow Central.

The church is a 10 minute walk from the station. If you think you need a lift from the church to barn let us know.

DRIVING

To the church
Exit the M6 J43. The church is on Warwick Road. There is free on-street parking nearby.

From the church to the reception
Take the A595 towards Cockermouth. Exit after Mealsgate at the sign for ‘Torpenhow’. Follow the road into Torpenhow. Make a left at the t-junction. On leaving the village look for a lane on the left signposted ‘Park House’.

TAXIS

Here are a couple of local taxi firms for getting around easily. Give them a call:

Accomodation

You can glamp or camp at the farm, stay nearby or closer to Rosies house in Raughton Head for tea and cake the next day. There are hotels in Carlisle and Penrith too which could be handier for catching trains. Please contact us if you're interested in staying on site.

ON SITE

There are x2 2-person shepherds huts and a 4-person hut. They have heating and electic. £90 per hut incl breakfast.

A yurt with double bed for 2 people with wood burner but no power. £90 per night incl breakfast.

A bell tent with no power or heaters (but looks nice!) sleeps 4 people. £35 per night incl breakfast.

Space for tents and vans is also available at £20 a pitch incl breakfast.
